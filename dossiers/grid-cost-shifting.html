BLACK MOUNTAIN DATA CENTER CONTINUANCE & GRID LOAD

VERIFIED BY PUBLIC RECORD
⚖️ AUDIT VERDICT: VERIFIED

Public regulatory filings confirm that proposed substation capacity enhancements and municipal utility extensions for high-density computing loads shift upfront infrastructure capital expenditures onto general residential rate classes unless specific anti-cost-shifting tariff riders are enforced. An inquiry is not an adjudication.

1. The Case Detail

The proposed data center development by Black Mountain Power (Zoning Case ZC-26-041) covers approximately 187 acres on Lon Stephenson Road, located in Fort Worth's Council District 8. The developer has petitioned for an amendment to Planned Development 1420 to permit the construction of a dedicated high-capacity electrical substation and to increase building height limits from 55 to 70 feet to accommodate high-density server rack infrastructure.

Additionally, public disclosures reveal substantial financial contributions from developer entities to state and municipal officials concurrent with zoning applications. While the developer has run localized promotional campaigns claiming the project funds critical infrastructure without taxpayer expense, analysis of utility regulations indicates standard grid connection protocols shift these costs back to ratepayers.

2. Documentary Evidence & Inline Sources

A review of public campaign finance records reveals significant political contributions from Rhett Bennett (associated with Black Mountain Power) to Governor Greg Abbott totaling $500,000. Municipal records show additional campaign finance contributions totaling approximately $46,000 to the Mayor and eight city council members during relevant election cycles.

On June 10, 2026, Governor Abbott issued a directive to the Public Utility Commission (PUCT) and ERCOT stating that large-scale data center developments must self-fund their grid-interconnection infrastructure to protect consumers. However, the directive schedules the joint PUCT/ERCOT implementation memo for July 17, 2026—weeks after the Fort Worth City Council's scheduled action on ZC-26-041.
